Still have Doubts?

We have answered some of the frequent questions for you!

When should I have my first gynecological visit?

Ideally, around age 13-15 for general health discussions or when sexually active. Regular exams, including Pap smears, start at 21.

How often should I visit a gynecologist?

Annually for routine checkups, Pap smears, and screenings. More frequent visits if you have specific concerns.

What should I expect during my first visit?

A general discussion about menstrual health, sexual activity, contraception, and preventive screenings. A physical exam may be included.

Are irregular periods normal?

Occasional irregular cycles are common, but persistent irregularity could indicate hormonal imbalance, PCOS, or other conditions.

What happens during a gynecological exam?

A typical exam includes a pelvic exam, Pap smear (if needed), breast exam, and discussion of any concerns.

How do I know if I have an STD?

Symptoms may include unusual discharge, itching, pain, or no symptoms at all. Regular testing is important.

What can help with painful periods?

Pain relievers, heat therapy, lifestyle changes, or hormonal birth control may help.
